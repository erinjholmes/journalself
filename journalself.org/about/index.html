We Design And Develop Software Protocols For Academic Publishing

JournalSelf is an open access framework that enables researchers to organize, write, review, and archive scientific papers, presentations, and other digital artifacts.

Signup for beta

Objectives, Mission, And Initiatives

Our objective is to support the work of academics in ways that enable the production, availability and accessibility of research. We aim to link research outcomes such as publications, data, and software to the researchers, institutions, and funders who create these bodies of work. Additionally, JournalSelf technology protocols lower costs and barriers to entry in publishing and accessing scholarly work.

Our mission is to create a publishing platform where information can be accessed; this is a movement of people and ideas who believe that knowledge should be open and freely shared. JournalSelf aims to be a low-cost alternative to producing scholarly data that is accessible and available by providing access to publishing tools that are powerful and effective.

JournalSelf is designed to advance publication infrastructure and optimize it for the needs of research and scholarship. We believe that a stable, competitive, and quality open access publishing system offers an immense benefit not only to scholarship but to society as a whole.

What We Strive For

JournalSelf is an academic publishing protocol in evolution, designed to provide access to peer-reviewed, and scholarly research papers for analysis, and productive re-use. We are committed to building protocols that enhance the accessibility and interoperability of academic publishing.

Customers & Shareholders

We view our stakeholders as the funders, societies, researchers, and people who benefit from scientific discoveries produced from these bodies of work.

Democratization

We believe in the merits of enabling authors to publish their research with minimal financial, organizational, legal, and technical barriers. Inclusive environments enhance the academic landscape.

Expanding Ecosystems

We value the intrinsic merits of interoperable infrastructures for the publication of research by software tool development, content provision, and universal metadata.

Distribution Of Work

We enable maximum reuse of published materials with full attribution to enable the widest sharing, re-use, and computational analysis of scholarly literature.
